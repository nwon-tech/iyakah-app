Misinformation and Disinformation
Misinformation: False information spread inadvertently without intent to harm.
In simpler words, misinformation is fake news that is created and spread by mistake ‚Äî by someone who doesn't realise that it's false.

Disinformation: False information spread deliberately to mislead and manipulate truth.

IYAKAH TIPS
The Fact Is
People believe and spread misinformation due to mental shortcuts, emotional responses, and alignment with personal beliefs. Strong emotions like fear or anger increase gullibility, while political bias and emotional states affect judgment. Some share misinformation to connect socially or gain attention. Social media algorithms worsen the problem by promoting similar content once users engage with it, creating a feedback loop. Even attempts to debunk falsehoods can amplify them. Disinformation, unlike misinformation, is spread intentionally for political, financial, or ideological goals to influence large audiences.
IYAKAH TIPS
Case in Study
The COVID-19 pandemic exposed the dangers of misinformation and disinformation. Early false claims about cures, vaccine risks, and virus origins spread online, amplifying confusion as governments struggled to understand the virus. Influencers and media figures worsened the situation. A 2022 CDC review acknowledged that unclear messaging added to public confusion. A 2023 JAMA study found that one-third of COVID-19 deaths in the U.S. could have been prevented with accurate health guidance. This highlights how misinformation fuels vaccine hesitancy, public mistrust, and preventable deaths, showing its severe impact on public health and safety.

Understanding Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks involve tricking individuals into revealing sensitive information through fraudulent emails, websites, or messages. These scams often impersonate trusted entities to steal personal data like passwords and banking credentials. Recognizing the signs of phishing and taking precautions can help protect against these threats.

IYAKAH TIPS
What is a Fake Website
A fake website is any website designed to deliberately mislead, deceive, or manipulate users by pretending to be something it is not. These sites often impersonate legitimate brands, organizations, or services to steal information, money, or trust.

IYAKAH TIPS
Check and Check
1. Check the URL: Look for misspelled domains, unusual domain extensions, extra characters, deceptive subdomains, or embedded brand names.
2. Check site security: Ensure the site uses TLS/SSL certificates. Types include DV (minimal validation), OV (business-verified), and EV (high-level verification). Fraudsters often use DV certificates.
3. Check domain age: New domains may indicate scams, as scammers often register short-term domains to reduce costs.
4. Check for errors: Scammers often have spelling or grammatical mistakes, poor design, and missing info (e.g., no contact details or ‚ÄúAbout Us‚Äù page).

Identify Deepfake Images

Deepfake images can be convincingly manipulated, making it crucial to recognize signs of AI-generated content. Learn how to spot inconsistencies, facial distortions, and other visual clues that can help distinguish fake images from genuine ones. Understanding these techniques is essential in combating misinformation.

IYAKAH TIPS
The Response
AI-generated images are visuals created by artificial intelligence algorithms without using real cameras or physical scenes. They can be realistic or abstract and convey themes or messages. AI can enhance existing photos by correcting issues like red eyes or improving sharpness and contrast. Alternatively, AI can create entirely new images using generators that combine various elements to produce original artworks or unique perspectives. These images are produced by machine learning models such as GANs or text-to-image systems like DALL¬∑E, Midjourney, and Stable Diffusion, which generate visuals from input data or text prompts.
IYAKAH TIPS
Be Careful
AI-generated and deepfake images have both positive and negative implications. On the positive side, they enable creativity in fields like art, entertainment, and education, providing innovative tools for content creation. However, they can also be misused to create misleading or harmful content, such as fake news, fraud, and privacy violations. The ability to manipulate images convincingly raises concerns about trust and authenticity in digital media. While AI advancements offer exciting possibilities, they also require ethical considerations and regulation to prevent misuse and ensure they are used responsibly.

Remain Skeptical
1. Watch out for misplaced content
Check for missing body parts, incorrect shadows, or nonsensical text that indicate AI-generated image flaws.
2. Be suspicious of smooth features
Look for unnaturally glossy skin, overly smooth textures, or blurred backgrounds common in AI images.
3. Check for watermarks
Some AI images include small logos or text showing they were created by AI tools.
4. Flip It and Reverse It
Use reverse image search to verify image authenticity, especially if it's viral but not on trusted sites.

"Identifying Media Bias

Learn how to recognize bias in media coverage by understanding the subtle ways information is presented. This guide explores different techniques used to detect bias, helping you critically evaluate news and avoid being misled.

IYAKAH TIPS
What is a News Bias
News Bias is defined as the tendency of news media to report in a way that reinforces a viewpoint, preference or political ideology, instead of reporting the news objectively. A media outlet may reveal bias in how it reports specific news stories or which stories they choose to cover, i.e., deem more important than others to cover or emphasize.
IYAKAH TIPS
Learn About
Left-Wing and Right-Wing are terms used to describe opposing political ideologies:
‚Ä¢ Left-wing politics generally support social equality, government intervention in the economy, and progressive values like environmental protection, minority rights, and wealth redistribution. It focuses on collective welfare and reducing inequality.
‚Ä¢ Right-wing politics typically favor free markets, individual responsibility, limited government, and traditional values such as family, religion, and national pride. It emphasizes personal freedom and economic growth with less state interference.
IYAKAH TIPS
Don't Ignore
News bias can significantly impact public perception and decision-making. When media outlets present information with a particular slant or omit key details, it shapes how audiences interpret events, often reinforcing existing beliefs or creating polarization. This bias can lead to misinformed opinions, as people may only receive one-sided perspectives. Over time, biased reporting erodes trust in the media and undermines the quality of public discourse. In political contexts, news bias can influence elections, as voters may be swayed by skewed portrayals of candidates or policies, ultimately affecting democratic processes and social unity.
IYAKAH TIPS
Get Ready
To start by examining the source. Consider the outlet's political alignment or past tendencies to favor certain viewpoints. Next, check the language used; biased reporting often includes emotionally charged or loaded words to influence readers. Look for the omission of key facts or one-sided reporting that fails to present multiple perspectives. Analyze the framing of stories‚Äîhow events are presented can shape perception. Additionally, cross-check information with other reputable sources to see if different outlets offer balanced views. Recognizing news bias requires critical thinking and awareness of the subtle ways media can shape opinions.
